GCV vs PCV — What's the Difference?

Commercial vehicle insurance splits into two broad categories based on what the vehicle carries — goods or people. The category decides how your premium is calculated.

🚛 GCV — Goods Carrying Vehicle

Trucks, tempos, lorries, mini-trucks, and pickups used to transport goods. Premium is based on Gross Vehicle Weight (GVW) — heavier vehicles fall into higher premium slabs. Further split into Public Carrier (goods transported for hire/reward, e.g. transport companies) and Private Carrier (goods transported for your own business use only, not for hire).

🚌 PCV — Passenger Carrying Vehicle

Taxis, cabs, auto-rickshaws, school buses, staff buses, and stage-carriage buses used to transport people. Premium is based on seating capacity and vehicle type. Categories include private cars used commercially (cab aggregators), 3-wheeler auto-rickshaws, and buses (school/staff/route permit).

What Decides Your Premium

For GCV (Goods Vehicles)

Gross Vehicle Weight (GVW) is the primary factor — IRDAI groups goods vehicles into weight slabs (commonly cited bands include up to 7,500 kg, mid-range, and above 20,000 kg), with higher GVW meaning higher third-party premium. Whether the vehicle is a Public Carrier (for hire) or Private Carrier (own use) also changes the applicable rate.

For PCV (Passenger Vehicles)

Seating capacity is the primary factor for buses and vans — more seats generally means higher premium. Taxis/cabs and auto-rickshaws have their own separate rate categories, distinct from private (non-commercial) car or two-wheeler rates.

⚠️ IRDAI revises commercial vehicle third-party rates periodically, and exact slab-wise figures change with each notification — sometimes with different rates for different states/zones. Because these rates depend on your exact GVW, seating capacity, and carrier type, we don't publish a fixed rate table here. How Buying or Renewing Actually Works

Confirm Your Exact Category

Know whether your vehicle is GCV or PCV, its GVW (for goods vehicles) or seating capacity (for passenger vehicles), and whether it's registered as Public or Private carrier — this is on your RC.

Decide Third Party Only or Comprehensive

Third Party is the mandatory minimum. Comprehensive adds Own Damage cover — strongly worth considering for commercial vehicles given their higher usage and accident exposure.

Keep Documents Ready

RC (showing GVW/seating and carrier type), permit copy (for public carriers), previous policy copy if renewing, and driver details for PA cover.

Compare Own Damage Quotes

Third party rates are fixed and identical across insurers, but Own Damage/Comprehensive premiums vary — worth comparing 2-3 insurers, especially for fleet or higher-value commercial vehicles.

Renew Before Expiry

A commercial vehicle running without valid insurance risks heavy fines and impounding — renew before the expiry date, every time, without exception.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between GCV and PCV insurance?

GCV (Goods Carrying Vehicle) insurance covers vehicles that transport goods — trucks, tempos, lorries — with premium based mainly on Gross Vehicle Weight. PCV (Passenger Carrying Vehicle) insurance covers vehicles that transport people — taxis, autos, buses — with premium based mainly on seating capacity and vehicle type.

What's the difference between Public Carrier and Private Carrier?

A Public Carrier goods vehicle transports goods for hire or reward — i.e., you're paid to carry other people's goods, typically requiring a permit. A Private Carrier transports goods only for your own business use, not for hire. This distinction affects both the permit required and the applicable premium rate.

Is third party insurance mandatory for commercial vehicles?

Yes. Section 146 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 makes third party insurance mandatory for every commercial vehicle on Indian roads, same as private vehicles. Operating a commercial vehicle without valid third party cover risks fines and potential impounding — the financial and legal exposure is typically higher than for private vehicles given commercial usage.

Can I negotiate the third party premium for my truck or taxi?

No. Third party premium rates for commercial vehicles are fixed by IRDAI based on GVW (goods vehicles) or seating capacity/vehicle type (passenger vehicles), and are identical across every insurer. Only the Own Damage/Comprehensive portion of your premium is negotiable and varies by insurer.

Do I need a permit in addition to insurance?

Yes, separately from insurance. Commercial vehicles used for hire (public carriers, taxis, buses on route) require a valid permit from the regional transport authority. Insurance and permit are two separate legal requirements — both must be valid and current.

Should I buy third party only or comprehensive for a commercial vehicle?

Given commercial vehicles typically see higher daily usage and accident exposure than private vehicles, comprehensive cover (which adds Own Damage protection) is generally worth the extra cost — particularly for higher-value trucks, buses, or vehicles central to your business income.
