The Basics

GCV vs PCV — What's the Difference?

Commercial vehicle insurance splits into two broad categories based on what the vehicle carries — goods or people. The category decides how your premium is calculated.

🚛 GCV — Goods Carrying Vehicle

Trucks, tempos, lorries, mini-trucks, and pickups used to transport goods. Premium is based on Gross Vehicle Weight (GVW) — heavier vehicles fall into higher premium slabs. Further split into Public Carrier (goods transported for hire/reward) and Private Carrier (goods transported for your own business use only, not for hire).

🚌 PCV — Passenger Carrying Vehicle

Taxis, cabs, auto-rickshaws, school buses, staff buses, and stage-carriage buses used to transport people. Premium is based on seating capacity and vehicle type. Categories include private cars used commercially (cab aggregators), 3-wheeler auto-rickshaws, and buses (school/staff/route permit).

Premium Factors

What Decides Your Premium

For GCV (Goods Vehicles)

Gross Vehicle Weight (GVW) is the primary factor — IRDAI groups goods vehicles into weight slabs (commonly cited bands include up to 7,500 kg, mid-range, and above 20,000 kg), with higher GVW meaning higher third-party premium. Whether the vehicle is a Public Carrier or Private Carrier also changes the applicable rate.

For PCV (Passenger Vehicles)

Seating capacity is the primary factor for buses and vans — more seats generally means higher premium. Taxis/cabs and auto-rickshaws have their own separate rate categories, distinct from private (non-commercial) car or two-wheeler rates.

⚠️ IRDAI revises commercial vehicle third-party rates periodically, and exact slab-wise figures change with each notification — sometimes with different rates for different states/zones. Because these rates depend on your exact GVW, seating capacity, and carrier type, we don't publish a fixed rate table here. The Process

How Buying or Renewing Actually Works

Confirm Your Exact Category

Know whether your vehicle is GCV or PCV, its GVW (for goods vehicles) or seating capacity (for passenger vehicles), and whether it's registered as Public or Private carrier — this is on your RC.

Decide Third Party Only or Comprehensive

Third Party is the mandatory minimum. Comprehensive adds Own Damage cover — strongly worth considering for commercial vehicles given their higher usage and accident exposure.

Keep Documents Ready

RC (showing GVW/seating and carrier type), permit copy (for public carriers), previous policy copy if renewing, and driver details for PA cover.

Compare Own Damage Quotes

Third party rates are fixed and identical across insurers, but Own Damage/Comprehensive premiums vary — worth comparing 2-3 insurers, especially for fleet or higher-value commercial vehicles.

Renew Before Expiry

A commercial vehicle running without valid insurance risks heavy fines and impounding — renew before the expiry date, every time, without exception.
