The Basics

GCV vs PCV — What's the Difference?

Commercial vehicle insurance splits into two broad categories based on what the vehicle carries — goods or people. The category decides how your premium is calculated.

🚛 GCV — Goods Carrying Vehicle

Trucks, tempos, lorries, mini-trucks, and pickups used to transport goods. Premium is based on Gross Vehicle Weight (GVW) — heavier vehicles fall into higher premium slabs. Further split into Public Carrier (goods transported for hire/reward) and Private Carrier (goods transported for your own business use only).

🚌 PCV — Passenger Carrying Vehicle

Taxis, cabs, auto-rickshaws, school buses, staff buses, and stage-carriage buses. Premium is based on seating capacity and vehicle type. Taxis/cabs and auto-rickshaws have their own separate rate categories, distinct from private (non-commercial) car or two-wheeler rates.

Third-party is mandatory for every commercial vehicle regardless of type. Comprehensive is the real decision — and for a working vehicle, the case for it is usually stronger than for a private car.

No Claim Bonus

Your NCB Ladder — And Why It's Worth Protecting

No Claim Bonus is a discount on your own-damage premium for every claim-free year — it doesn't apply to the mandatory third-party portion. It resets to zero the moment you make an own-damage claim. This slab structure is set by IRDAI and applies the same way across two-wheelers, cars, and commercial vehicles alike.

Two Separate Requirements

Insurance and Permit Are Not the Same Thing

This trips up a lot of commercial vehicle owners — having valid insurance does not mean you're legally clear to operate for hire.

A permit from the regional transport authority is required separately from insurance for any commercial vehicle used for hire — public carrier goods vehicles, taxis, autos, and route buses. Insurance covers financial liability; the permit is your legal authorization to operate commercially. Both must be valid at all times, and neither substitutes for the other.

If you're a Private Carrier (goods vehicle used only for your own business, not for hire), permit requirements differ — worth confirming your exact category with us before assuming either way.

What Actually Moves Your Premium

What Insurers Actually Check

Gross Vehicle Weight (GCV)

Third-party rates step up in weight slabs set by IRDAI — heavier vehicles cost more to insure. Whether you're a Public or Private Carrier also changes the applicable rate.

Seating Capacity (PCV)

Buses and vans are priced largely by seating capacity. Taxis and auto-rickshaws sit in their own distinct rate categories.

Usage Intensity

A vehicle in daily commercial use faces materially higher accident exposure than an occasional or backup vehicle — this matters most for whether comprehensive is worth it.

Claims History

A recent own-damage claim affects your No Claim Bonus on the comprehensive portion, same as private vehicles — this is worth checking before your next repair decision.

Said Plainly, Once

Why There's No Rate Table On This Page

Commercial vehicle third-party rates are genuinely more variable than private car or bike rates — and change more often.

Third-party rates for commercial vehicles are fixed by IRDAI based on your exact Gross Vehicle Weight or seating capacity, and are revised periodically — sometimes with meaningful gaps between what's commonly cited online and what's currently in force.
