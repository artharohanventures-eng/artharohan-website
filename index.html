    Loan Types

    Which Loan Do You Need?

    Every self-employed business has different needs. Understanding which loan fits your situation is the first step — and the most important one.

    What is it?

    An unsecured business loan gives you a lump sum amount for business purposes — expansion, equipment, inventory, or cash flow — without pledging any property or asset as security. The lender evaluates your business income, ITR, and credit score instead of collateral.

    Who is it for?

    • Shop owners and retailers in Delhi NCR
    • Traders and wholesalers
    • Doctors, CA, architects, consultants
    • Contractors and service businesses
    • Proprietors and partnership firms

    Eligibility

    • Age: 21–65 years
    • Minimum 2–3 years in business
    • ITR filed for at least 2 years
    • CIBIL score 700+ preferred
    • Minimum annual turnover as per lender

    Documents Required

    • PAN Card and Aadhaar Card
    • Last 2 years ITR with computation
    • 6–12 months bank statements
    • Business proof (GST, trade licence)
    • Shop/office address proof

    Important: Interest rates and loan amounts vary by lender, your credit profile, and business vintage. The numbers above are general ranges. Artharohan helps you understand your options — we do not guarantee specific rates or approvals.

    What is it?

    Working capital finance is specifically designed to fund the day-to-day operations of your business — purchasing raw material, managing inventory, paying staff, or bridging payment gaps between billing and collection. It is short-term in nature and typically revolving.

    Who is it for?

    • Traders and distributors with seasonal demand
    • Manufacturers needing raw material funding
    • Retailers managing festive season inventory
    • Service businesses with long payment cycles
    • Any business with a gap between income and expense

    Types Available

    • Cash Credit (CC) — revolving credit from bank
    • Working Capital Term Loan — fixed EMI structure
    • Invoice Discounting — advance against invoices
    • Unsecured Working Capital — no collateral needed
    • Supply Chain Finance — for vendor/dealer networks

    Key Things to Know

    • Cash Credit is the most common form for traders
    • Interest charged only on amount used in CC
    • Working capital is not for asset purchase
    • Bank statements showing regular business cash flow are critical
    • GST returns help establish business credibility

    What is it?

    A Credit Limit or Overdraft (OD) facility allows you to withdraw money beyond your bank account balance up to a pre-approved limit. It is a flexible financing tool — you use what you need, when you need it, and pay interest only on the amount used and the days used.

    Credit Limit vs OD — The Difference

    • OD is linked to your current account with a bank
    • Credit Limit is a standalone revolving line
    • Both let you borrow, repay, and borrow again
    • OD typically requires property as security
    • Unsecured credit limits are available for good CIBIL profiles

    Who benefits most?

    • Businesses with unpredictable cash flow timing
    • Self-employed with irregular income patterns
    • Traders who need short-term liquidity repeatedly
    • Professionals who need emergency funds for business
    • Anyone who wants a credit cushion without a term loan

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    • Using OD for personal expenses — reduces business credibility
    • Not repaying regularly — hurts CIBIL and renewal chances
    • Taking larger limit than needed — unnecessary interest cost
    • Confusing OD interest with term loan EMI — very different

    What is Mudra Loan?

    Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) is a Government of India scheme providing loans up to ₹20 Lakh to non-corporate, non-farm small and micro enterprises. It is provided through banks, NBFCs, and MFIs. No collateral required under ₹10 Lakh.

    Three Categories

    • Shishu: Up to ₹50,000 — for startups and new businesses
    • Kishor: ₹50,001 to ₹5 Lakh — for established small businesses
    • Tarun: ₹5 Lakh to ₹20 Lakh — for growing businesses
    • New category: Tarun Plus — up to ₹20 Lakh for Tarun repayers

    Who Can Apply?

    • Small shopkeepers, vegetable vendors, artisans
    • Service sector — salons, tailors, repair shops
    • Small manufacturers, food processing units
    • Traders in agriculture-allied activities
    • Self-employed professionals with small businesses

    Reality Check — What Banks Actually See

    • Mudra does not guarantee loan approval — bank still evaluates you
    • Low CIBIL scores still result in rejection
    • Business proof and bank statements are required
    • No agent or consultant fee is required for Mudra — it is free to apply
    • Apply directly at your bank or via Jan Samarth Portal

    Khabardar: No legitimate Mudra Loan agent charges upfront fees. If anyone asks for processing fees before loan approval — that is fraud. Walk away and report it to your bank.

    The Process

    How Business Loan Application Actually Works

    Most rejections happen because people apply without understanding the process. Here is the complete picture — step by step.

    01

    Understand Your Need

    Before approaching any lender, be clear on three things: how much you need, what you need it for, and how long you need it for. Lenders ask these questions. Having clear answers improves approval chances and helps you pick the right loan type.

    02

    Check Your CIBIL Score

    Your CIBIL score is the first thing every lender checks. Scores below 700 significantly reduce your chances. Check your score for free on the CIBIL website or via your bank's app before applying anywhere. If it's low — understand why before applying, not after rejection.

    03

    Prepare Your Documents

    Most applications fail at this stage — not because of eligibility, but because documents are incomplete or inconsistent. Your ITR, bank statements, and GST returns should tell the same story about your business income. Discrepancies between these documents are the most common reason for rejection.

    04

    Choose the Right Lender

    Banks offer lower interest rates but stricter eligibility. NBFCs are faster and more flexible but charge higher rates. Fintech lenders are fastest but most expensive. The right choice depends on your urgency, CIBIL score, and loan amount. Comparing at least 3 lenders before deciding is the minimum standard.

    05

    Apply and Track

    Once submitted, follow up every 3–4 working days. Know your application reference number. If the lender asks for additional documents, respond within 24 hours — delays at your end can result in the application being cancelled. Track processing timelines and escalate if they exceed the promised timeline.

    06

    Read Before You Sign

    Read the sanction letter completely. Check the interest rate (flat vs reducing), processing fee, prepayment penalty, and foreclosure charges. The difference between a flat rate and reducing rate loan on the same amount can mean paying significantly more interest over the tenure. Never sign under pressure.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Business Loan Questions — Answered Honestly

    Questions that self-employed individuals in Delhi NCR ask most often — with straight answers, no sales pitch.

    Most banks and NBFCs require at least 1–2 years of ITR for business loan approval. Without ITR, your options are very limited — some fintech lenders offer loans based on bank statement analysis or GST turnover, but these typically come with much higher interest rates and smaller amounts. Filing ITR consistently is the single most important thing a self-employed person can do to improve loan eligibility.
    Most lenders in Delhi NCR prefer a CIBIL score of 700 or above for business loans. NBFCs may consider scores of 650–699 with higher interest rates. Scores below 650 make approval very difficult with formal lenders. It is strongly advisable to check and improve your CIBIL score before applying rather than accumulating rejections, which further damage the score.
    No. Unsecured business loans do not require property, gold, or any other collateral. The lender relies entirely on your credit profile, income documents, business vintage, and repayment capacity. However, for loan amounts above ₹25–30 Lakh, some lenders may still ask for collateral or a guarantor depending on your credit profile.
    Timelines vary significantly by lender. NBFCs and fintech lenders can sanction in 3–7 working days with complete documents. Banks typically take 10–20 working days due to more rigorous processing. Government scheme loans like Mudra can take longer due to bank processing queues. The most common cause of delay is incomplete or inconsistent documentation at the applicant's end.
    No legitimate lender or DSA (Direct Selling Agent) charges any upfront fee before loan sanction. A processing fee is only charged after sanction and is typically deducted from the disbursed amount — not collected separately before disbursement. If anyone asks you for cash payment, UPI transfer, or advance fee before the loan is approved — that is a fraud. Stop all engagement and report it.
    Yes. Both proprietorship firms and partnership firms are eligible for business loans. Proprietorships typically submit the owner's personal documents along with business proof. Partnership firms need to provide a partnership deed in addition to the standard documents. Private Limited Companies have a separate application process and eligibility criteria.
    A business loan is typically a term loan — you receive a lump sum and repay in fixed EMIs over a defined period. A working capital loan is designed to fund ongoing business operations and may be structured as a revolving credit line (Cash Credit or Overdraft). Working capital facilities are more flexible but require active management. The right choice depends on whether your need is one-time (term loan) or ongoing and recurring (working capital).
