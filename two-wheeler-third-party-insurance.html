What Third Party Insurance Actually Covers

Third party (TP) insurance protects others, not your own bike. If your two-wheeler causes injury, death, or property damage to someone else, this policy pays for it — up to the legal limits set by the Motor Vehicles Act.

✅ What's Covered

Injury or death to a third party. Property damage to a third party's vehicle or belongings, up to ₹1 lakh. Legal liability as decided by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal.

❌ What's Not Covered

Damage to your own bike — theft, fire, accident, or natural calamity. For that, you need Own Damage (OD) cover or a Comprehensive policy.

How Buying Actually Works

Know What You Need

Decide between pure Third Party (cheapest, legally sufficient) or Comprehensive (TP + own damage — recommended if your bike is worth more than ₹50,000 or under 5 years old).

Keep Your Documents Ready

RC (Registration Certificate), previous policy copy (if renewing), and your No Claim Bonus (NCB) details if applicable.

Compare Before You Buy

TP premium is fixed — but OD/comprehensive premiums differ between insurers. Comparing 2-3 options before buying is worth the 10 minutes.

Renew On Time

Don't let your policy lapse. If you miss renewal by more than 90 days, you lose your accumulated No Claim Bonus — that discount doesn't come back.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is third party insurance really mandatory?

Yes. Section 146 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 makes it a legal requirement for every vehicle on Indian roads. Riding without it can attract a fine of ₹2,000 for a first offence, ₹4,000 for repeat offences, and possible imprisonment.

Can I negotiate the third party premium?

No. TP premium rates are fixed annually by IRDAI based on engine capacity and are identical across every insurance company in India. Only Own Damage and Comprehensive premiums vary by insurer.

My bike is 4 years old — do I still need to renew separately?

If you bought your bike new after September 2018, it likely came with a mandatory 5-year third party policy bundled in. Check your policy document for the expiry date — once that 5-year term ends, you'll need to renew annually going forward.

What happens if I let my policy lapse?

You lose legal cover immediately — riding becomes an offence. If you renew within 90 days, your No Claim Bonus is usually protected. Beyond that, you typically lose the accumulated NCB discount.

Should I buy third party only, or comprehensive?

If your bike is old with low resale value, third party alone may be enough. If it's under 5 years old or worth more than ₹50,000-60,000, comprehensive is strongly advisable — the extra cost is usually small compared to the protection it adds.
