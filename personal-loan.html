Before You Commit

Cheaper Options Worth Checking First

A personal loan is unsecured, which is exactly why it usually costs more than options where you already have an asset backing it. If any of these apply to you, they're worth a phone call before you commit to a personal loan.

Loan Against Fixed Deposit

If you have an FD, a loan against it typically costs 1-2% above your FD rate — often far cheaper than an unsecured personal loan, and your FD keeps earning interest.

Gold Loan

If you have gold jewelry, gold loans usually carry lower rates than personal loans and faster disbursal, since the lender's risk is much lower.

Top-Up on Existing Home Loan

If you already have a home loan running, a top-up loan is usually priced close to your home loan rate — meaningfully cheaper than a fresh personal loan.

Credit Card EMI Conversion

For smaller amounts, converting a purchase to EMI on an existing card can sometimes beat personal loan rates — worth comparing before applying fresh.

We'll ask if you have any of these before we quote you a personal loan. It may mean less business for us — but a call where we tell you about a cheaper option is worth more to you than a loan we could've sold you.

Eligibility

What Lenders Actually Check

For Salaried Applicants

  • Minimum monthly income usually ₹20,000-25,000 in Delhi NCR, varies by lender
  • Stable employment, typically 1+ year at current job
  • CIBIL score 700+ preferred for best rates
  • Salary slips (last 3 months) and bank statements as proof

For Self-Employed Applicants

  • ITR for last 2 years typically required
  • Business vintage of 2-3 years generally expected
  • CIBIL score matters more here — no collateral to fall back on
  • Bank statement cash flow reviewed closely, GST returns may help

What Affects Your Loan Amount

  • Lenders typically cap total EMIs (existing + new) at 40-50% of monthly income
  • Existing personal loans or credit card dues reduce what you're eligible for
  • Higher CIBIL can mean a larger sanctioned amount, not just a better rate

Documents Needed (Both)

  • PAN Card and Aadhaar Card
  • Income proof (salary slips or ITR)
  • Bank statements — usually last 6 months
  • Address proof and passport-size photo
Honest Before Anything Else

When We'll Tell You "Not Yet"

Personal loans are the easiest loan to over-borrow with, because there's no asset stopping you. If any of these apply to you, we'll say so before you waste time applying — even though it means no business for us that day.

⚠️ You Already Have 2+ Running Personal Loans

Stacking personal loans is one of the fastest routes to a debt trap. We'll flag this honestly and talk through consolidation or a pause, rather than push a third loan.

⚠️ CIBIL Below 650

Approval becomes difficult and rates get steep with most lenders. We'll tell you this upfront and suggest what to fix first, rather than route you to a high-cost NBFC that may approve it anyway.

⚠️ It's to Pay Off Another Loan, Without Fixing the Cause

Using a personal loan to cover another debt can work as consolidation — but only if the underlying spending pattern is addressed too. We'll ask this question honestly before recommending it.

⚠️ EMI Would Cross 40-45% of Income

Even if a lender approves it, an EMI at this level leaves little room for emergencies. We'll say so, and the calculator above will flag it too.

The Process

How Personal Loan Approval Actually Works

Know Your Real Reason First

Personal loans are flexible — which also makes them easy to justify for things that don't need debt. Be clear on why you need it before shopping rates.

Check Cheaper Alternatives

FD-backed loans, gold loans, or a home loan top-up are often cheaper than an unsecured personal loan — worth ten minutes to check before applying.

Check Your CIBIL Score

Your CIBIL score directly affects both your interest rate and your loan amount. A few months of clearing dues can meaningfully improve what you qualify for.

Compare Processing Fee & Foreclosure Charges

A lower headline rate with a high processing fee or steep prepayment penalty can cost more overall. Always ask for the total cost, not just the rate.

Watch for Bundled Insurance

Some lenders push a loan-protection insurance add-on at disbursal. It's optional in most cases — understand what it costs and whether you actually need it before agreeing.
