New Car Loan vs Used Car Loan

Financing a new car and financing a used car are structured very differently — different loan-to-value ratios, different tenures, and different lender risk assessment. Knowing the difference before you apply saves time and avoids surprises.

New Car Loan

  • Higher financing ratio — often up to 85-90% of on-road price
  • Longer tenure typically available — up to 7 years
  • Generally lower interest rates than used car loans
  • Faster approval — car's value is straightforward to assess
  • Wide lender choice — banks, NBFCs, dealer tie-ups

Used Car Loan

  • Lower financing ratio — typically 60-80% of assessed value
  • Shorter tenure — often capped around 5 years, sometimes less depending on car age
  • Interest rates usually higher than new car loans
  • Car's age, condition, and valuation directly affect eligibility
  • Fewer lenders specialize in used car financing — compare carefully

⚠️ Exact interest rates, financing percentage, and maximum tenure vary significantly by lender, your credit profile, and the specific car's age and valuation. The ranges above are general market patterns, not a quote. Message us your car details (new/used, age if used, approximate price) and we'll help you understand what to expect.

Eligibility

What Lenders Actually Check

For Salaried Applicants

  • Stable employment, usually minimum 1-2 years at current job
  • CIBIL score 700+ preferred for best rates
  • Salary slips and bank statements as income proof
  • Existing EMI obligations reduce eligible loan amount

For Self-Employed Applicants

  • ITR for last 2 years typically required
  • Business vintage and bank statement cash flow reviewed
  • CIBIL score matters just as much as for salaried applicants
  • Some lenders may ask for GST returns as supporting proof

For Used Cars Specifically

  • Car's age at loan maturity matters — most lenders cap total car age (e.g. car + tenure ≤ 8-10 years)
  • Vehicle valuation/inspection may be required
  • RC transfer and ownership history checked
  • Loan amount is based on assessed market value, not the sale price alone

Documents Needed (Both)

  • PAN Card and Aadhaar Card
  • Income proof (salary slips or ITR)
  • Bank statements — usually last 6 months
  • For used cars: RC copy, previous loan closure (if any), insurance copy
The Process

How Car Loan Approval Actually Works

Decide Your Budget First

Before shortlisting a car, know how much EMI fits comfortably into your monthly budget — not just what the maximum loan amount could be. A larger loan approval doesn't mean it's the right amount to take.

Check Your CIBIL Score

Your CIBIL score directly affects the interest rate you're offered. Check it before applying — a few months of clearing dues can meaningfully improve the rate you qualify for.

Compare Down Payment vs Loan Amount

A larger down payment reduces your EMI burden and total interest paid over the loan term. For used cars especially, a healthy down payment also improves approval chances given tighter financing ratios.

For Used Cars — Verify Before You Commit

Confirm the car's ownership history, accident record, and outstanding loan status (if any) before finalizing. A lender's valuation may also differ from the seller's asking price — know this before you're locked in.

Read the Loan Agreement Fully

Check interest rate type (flat vs reducing balance), processing fee, prepayment penalty, and foreclosure charges before signing. These terms materially affect your total cost over the loan tenure.
