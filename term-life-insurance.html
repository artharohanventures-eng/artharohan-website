Choosing Right

Term Plan vs Traditional vs ULIP

This is the real first decision — how much of your premium should go purely to protection versus a savings/investment component.

Term Plan

  • Pure protection, no investment component
  • Cheapest premium for highest cover
  • No maturity value if you survive the term
  • Best if your only goal is family protection

Traditional / Endowment

  • Combines insurance with guaranteed savings
  • Much higher premium for the same cover
  • Maturity payout if you survive the term
  • Lower cover amount for the same budget

ULIP

  • Combines insurance with market-linked investment
  • Returns depend on market performance
  • Higher charges than pure term or mutual funds
  • Better suited to those who want insurance + investment in one

⚠️ A common, costly mistake: buying a smaller endowment or ULIP policy because it "feels safer" since you get money back, when a term plan at the same premium could have given 5-10x the cover. If protection is the actual goal, keep it pure — invest the difference separately if you want growth.

Said Plainly, Once

Why There's No Premium Shown On This Page

Unlike motor third-party insurance, which has a government-fixed rate, term life premium has no regulator-fixed component at all.

Your actual premium depends on your exact age, gender, smoking status, health history, occupation, family medical history, and the specific insurer's underwriting — factors we don't control and can't price without a real application and, usually, a medical test. We are advisors, not an insurer with a live rating engine, so we don't publish a number we can't stand behind.

What we can do: help you work out how much cover you actually need, flag what's likely to affect your premium or eligibility before you apply, and connect you to real, verified quotes from actual insurers — compared by claim settlement ratio, not just price.
