The Basics

What Term Insurance Actually Covers

A term plan pays your nominee a lump sum if you pass away during the policy term. It has no investment component — that's exactly why it's the cheapest way to get meaningful cover.

✅ Covered

  • Death benefit paid to your nominee
  • Tax-free income replacement for your family
  • Covers outstanding loans and liabilities
  • Optional riders: critical illness, disability, accidental death

❌ Not Covered

  • No maturity payout if you outlive the term
  • No investment growth or returns
  • No partial withdrawal facility
  • Pre-existing conditions, unless disclosed and accepted

Choosing Right

Term Plan vs Traditional vs ULIP

Term Plan

  • Pure protection, no investment component
  • Cheapest premium for highest cover
  • No maturity value if you survive the term
  • Best if your only goal is family protection

Traditional / Endowment

  • Combines insurance with guaranteed savings
  • Much higher premium for same cover
  • Maturity payout if you survive the term
  • Lower cover amount for the same budget

ULIP

  • Combines insurance with market-linked investment
  • Returns depend on market performance
  • Higher charges than pure term or mutual funds
  • Better suited to those who want insurance + investment in one

You typically need PAN card, address proof, age proof, income proof (salary slips or ITR), and you will need to undergo a medical test for higher cover amounts. Existing health conditions must be disclosed honestly, as non-disclosure is a common reason for claim rejection.
Claim settlement ratio is the percentage of claims an insurer actually pays out of all claims received in a year. A higher ratio, ideally above 95 percent, indicates the insurer reliably pays claims. This is one of the most important factors to check before choosing a term insurance provider, often more important than the premium amount itself.
