Types of Business Loans

Which Business Loan Do You Need?

Every self-employed business has different requirements. Understanding which loan matches your situation is the first and most important step — before you approach any lender.

Unsecured Business Loan in Delhi NCR for Self-Employed

What Is It?

An unsecured business loan provides a lump sum for your business — expansion, equipment, inventory, or cash flow — without pledging any property or asset. The lender evaluates your business income, CIBIL score, ITR, and bank statements instead of asking for collateral.

Who Can Apply?

  • Shop owners and retailers in Delhi NCR
  • Traders and wholesalers — Chandni Chowk, Karol Bagh, Lajpat Rai
  • Professionals — doctors, CAs, architects, consultants
  • Contractors and service businesses across NCR
  • Proprietors and partnership firms in Delhi

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: 21–65 years at time of loan maturity
  • Minimum 2–3 years in current business
  • ITR filed for at least 2 consecutive years
  • CIBIL score 700 or above preferred
  • Business proof — GST registration or trade licence

Documents Required

  • PAN Card and Aadhaar Card
  • Last 2 years ITR with P&L and balance sheet
  • 6–12 months current account bank statements
  • GST registration certificate or trade licence
  • Business address proof

Important: Interest rates and loan amounts vary by lender, CIBIL score, and business vintage. The information above is general educational guidance. Artharohan helps you understand your options — we do not guarantee specific approvals, rates, or disbursement timelines.

Working Capital Loan in Delhi NCR — Self-Employed and Traders

What Is It?

Working capital finance funds the day-to-day operations of your business — buying raw material, managing inventory, paying staff, or bridging gaps between billing and collection. It is short-term in nature and typically revolving — you use it, repay it, and use it again.

Who Needs It?

  • Traders and distributors with seasonal demand spikes
  • Manufacturers needing raw material funding
  • Retailers managing festive season inventory
  • Service businesses with long payment cycles
  • Any business with a gap between income and expense dates

Types of Working Capital Finance

  • Cash Credit (CC) — revolving credit from bank
  • Working Capital Term Loan — fixed EMI structure
  • Invoice Discounting — advance against pending invoices
  • Unsecured Working Capital — no collateral needed
  • Overdraft against current account

Key Things to Know

  • Cash Credit is the most common structure for traders
  • Interest charged only on the amount used in CC
  • Working capital is not meant for asset purchase
  • Bank statements showing regular cash flow are critical
  • GST returns significantly strengthen the application

Credit Limit and Overdraft Facility for Self-Employed in Delhi

What Is It?

A Credit Limit or Overdraft (OD) facility lets you withdraw beyond your account balance up to a pre-approved limit. You use what you need, when you need it. Interest is charged only on the amount used — and only for the days it is used. No EMI. No fixed repayment schedule.

Credit Limit vs OD — The Difference

  • OD is linked to your bank's current account
  • Credit Limit is a standalone revolving line of credit
  • Both let you borrow, repay, and borrow again
  • OD traditionally requires property as security
  • Unsecured credit limits available for CIBIL 700+ profiles

Who Benefits Most?

  • Businesses with unpredictable or irregular cash flow
  • Self-employed with variable monthly income
  • Traders who need short-term liquidity repeatedly
  • Professionals who need emergency business funds
  • Anyone wanting a financial cushion without a term loan

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using OD for personal expenses — hurts business credibility
  • Not repaying regularly — damages CIBIL and renewal chances
  • Taking a limit larger than required — unnecessary interest cost
  • Confusing OD interest with EMI — very different structures

Mudra Loan in Delhi NCR — Shishu, Kishor, Tarun Explained

What Is Mudra Loan?

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) provides loans up to ₹20 Lakh to non-corporate, non-farm small and micro enterprises. Provided through banks, NBFCs, and MFIs across Delhi NCR. No collateral required for loans under ₹10 Lakh. Available for proprietors, self-employed, and small traders.

Three Mudra Categories

  • Shishu: Up to ₹50,000 — for new and early-stage businesses
  • Kishor: ₹50,001 to ₹5 Lakh — for established small businesses
  • Tarun: ₹5 Lakh to ₹20 Lakh — for growing businesses
  • Apply directly at your bank branch or via Jan Samarth Portal

Who Can Apply for Mudra Loan in Delhi?

  • Small shop owners, vendors, artisans, and traders
  • Service businesses — salons, tailors, repair shops
  • Small food processing and manufacturing units
  • Self-employed professionals running small businesses
  • Agriculture-allied activity businesses

Reality Check — What Banks Actually See

  • Mudra does not guarantee approval — bank still evaluates you
  • Low CIBIL score still results in rejection
  • Business proof and bank statements are required
  • There is NO fee to apply for Mudra Loan — it is free
  • Apply at your nearest bank branch directly

Khabardar — Mudra Loan Fraud: No agent or consultant charges any fee for Mudra Loan applications. It is a government scheme — applying is completely free. If anyone asks for upfront payment to "process your Mudra Loan" — that is fraud. Walk away immediately and file a complaint at your nearest bank or cybercrime portal.

Step-by-Step Process

Business Loan Kaise Milega — Complete Process

Most business loan rejections in Delhi NCR happen not because of ineligibility — but because people apply without understanding the process. Here is the complete picture, step by step.

01

Understand Your Exact Requirement

Before approaching any bank or NBFC in Delhi NCR, be clear on three things: how much you need, what exactly you need it for, and for how long. Lenders ask these questions — having clear, specific answers improves approval chances and helps select the right loan type. A working capital need and a business expansion need require different loan structures.

02

Check Your CIBIL Score First

Your CIBIL score is the first filter every lender applies. Scores below 700 significantly reduce approval chances for unsecured business loans in Delhi. Check your score for free via the CIBIL website or your bank's mobile app before applying anywhere. If your score is low — understand why and address it before submitting applications. Rejected applications further damage CIBIL scores.

03

Prepare Your Documents Carefully

Most business loan applications in Delhi NCR fail at the documentation stage — not because of ineligibility, but because documents are incomplete or inconsistent. Your ITR, bank statements, and GST returns must tell the same story about your business income. A discrepancy between ITR income and bank deposits is the single most common reason lenders in Delhi reject business loan applications.

04

Compare Lenders — Bank vs NBFC vs Fintech

Banks offer lower interest rates but stricter eligibility and longer processing. NBFCs are faster and more flexible but charge higher rates. Fintech lenders are the fastest to approve but most expensive for interest. The right choice depends on your urgency, CIBIL profile, and loan amount. Comparing at least 3 lenders before deciding is the minimum — never take the first offer.

05

Apply and Follow Up Actively

After submitting, note your application reference number and follow up every 3–4 working days. If the lender requests additional documents, respond within 24 hours — delays on your end can result in the application being closed. Lenders in Delhi NCR typically have processing timelines of 7–20 working days depending on the institution type.

06

Read the Sanction Letter Before Signing

Read the sanction letter completely before accepting. Check: interest rate type (flat vs reducing balance), processing fee, prepayment penalty, foreclosure charges, and insurance bundling. The difference between a flat rate and reducing balance rate on the same loan amount can mean paying 40–50% more interest. Never sign under time pressure or without understanding every term.

Why Loans Get Rejected

Business Loan Reject Kyon Hota Hai — 6 Real Reasons

Understanding why business loans get rejected in Delhi NCR is as important as knowing how to apply. Every rejection also damages your CIBIL score — so knowing these reasons in advance prevents unnecessary applications.

Low CIBIL Score

A score below 700 signals credit risk to lenders. Even one missed EMI or late credit card payment in the past 12 months can trigger rejection.

Fix: Check CIBIL, clear overdue payments, wait 3–6 months before applying.

ITR Not Filed or Too Low

Banks calculate loan eligibility based on your declared income in ITR. If income shown is too low — even if your actual business does well — the eligible loan amount will be low or zero.

Fix: File ITR accurately, reflect real business income, maintain 2 years of continuous filing.

Bank Statement vs ITR Mismatch

If your bank deposits show higher cash flow than what's declared in ITR, lenders get suspicious about undisclosed income. This is one of the most common rejection triggers in Delhi NCR.

Fix: Ensure ITR and bank statements tell a consistent story about income.

Business Too New

Most unsecured business loan lenders require 2–3 years of business vintage. A business younger than 2 years has very limited options, and those available come at significantly higher rates.

Fix: Explore Mudra Loan for newer businesses — more accessible for early-stage ventures.

High Existing Debt (FOIR)

Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio — if your current EMIs already consume 50–60% of your income, lenders won't add more debt load. Existing personal loans and credit card dues reduce your business loan eligibility.

Fix: Reduce existing EMI burden before applying for a new business loan.

Wrong Loan Structuring

Applying for a working capital loan when you actually need a term loan — or applying to a bank when an NBFC would be better suited — leads to rejection that could have been avoided with better guidance.

Fix: Get guidance on which loan type and which lender suits your specific profile before applying.
