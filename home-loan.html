Already Have a Home Loan?

Check This Before Anything Else

If you already have a running home loan, the highest-value conversation isn't about a new loan — it's whether your current rate is still competitive. Interest rates move; many existing borrowers are quietly paying 0.5-1% more than new customers at the same bank.

When Balance Transfer Is Worth It

  • Your current rate is 0.5%+ higher than what new borrowers get today
  • You have significant tenure remaining (transfer costs take longer to recover on short remaining terms)
  • Your CIBIL score has improved since you took the original loan

What It Actually Costs

  • Processing fee at the new lender, typically 0.5-1%
  • Some paperwork and property document re-verification
  • Foreclosure charges from your current lender — but RBI mandates zero foreclosure charges on floating-rate home loans for individuals

Tell us your current rate and outstanding balance — we'll do the actual math on whether switching saves you money, before recommending anything. Sometimes the honest answer is that it's not worth the paperwork.

Eligibility

What Lenders Actually Check

For Salaried Applicants

  • Stable employment, usually 2+ years including current job
  • Age at loan maturity typically capped around 60-65
  • CIBIL score 700+ preferred for best rates
  • Salary slips and bank statements as income proof

For Self-Employed Applicants

  • ITR for last 2-3 years typically required
  • Business vintage of 3+ years generally expected
  • Bank statement cash flow reviewed closely, GST returns help
  • CIBIL score still matters, though property collateral helps flexibility

What the Property Must Clear

  • Clear, undisputed title — no ongoing litigation
  • Approved building layout, RERA registration for under-construction projects
  • Lender-approved legal and technical valuation before disbursal
  • For resale property: chain of ownership documents verified

Documents Needed (Both)

  • PAN Card and Aadhaar Card
  • Income proof (salary slips or ITR)
  • Bank statements — usually last 6 months
  • Property documents — sale agreement, title deed, approved layout
Honest Before Anything Else

When We'll Tell You "Not Yet"

A home loan is a 15-30 year commitment. If any of these apply to you, we'll say so before you get emotionally attached to a property — even though it means no business for us that day.

⚠️ Property's Title or Legal Status Is Unclear

If there's ongoing litigation, an unclear title, or the project isn't RERA-registered, most lenders will reject or delay significantly. We'll flag this before you pay a booking amount you might not get back easily.

⚠️ CIBIL Below 650 With a Thin Credit File

Property collateral helps, but a very weak profile still means limited lenders and steep rates. We'll tell you honestly what to fix first, rather than force an application likely to be rejected.

⚠️ EMI Would Cross 40-45% of Income

Even if a lender approves a higher amount, an EMI at this level over 20-30 years leaves little room for emergencies, rate hikes, or life changes. We'll say so, and the calculator above will flag it too.

⚠️ You Haven't Checked Balance Transfer Yet (Existing Borrowers)

If you already have a home loan and haven't compared your current rate against today's market, that's the conversation to have first — before any other financial decision involving this property.

The Process

How Home Loan Approval Actually Works

Know Your Affordability Before You Shortlist

Decide your comfortable EMI and down payment budget before falling for a specific property — not after.

Verify the Property's Legal Clearance

Clear title, RERA registration (if under-construction), and approved layout — check this before paying any booking amount, not after.

Check Your CIBIL Score

Your CIBIL score directly affects your rate. A few months of clearing dues before applying can meaningfully improve what you're offered.

Compare Fixed vs Floating Rate

Floating rates typically track a repo-linked benchmark and can move with policy changes; fixed rates cost more upfront but stay predictable. Understand which fits your risk comfort.

Know Your Prepayment Rights

RBI mandates no foreclosure or prepayment charges on floating-rate home loans for individual borrowers — confirm this explicitly in your agreement.
