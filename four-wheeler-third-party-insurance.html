The Basics

What Third Party Car Insurance Actually Covers

Third party insurance protects you financially if your car causes injury, death, or property damage to someone else — it does not cover your own vehicle's damage.

✅ Covered

  • Injury or death caused to a third person
  • Damage to third-party vehicle or property
  • Legal liability arising from an accident you cause
  • Court-awarded compensation, up to policy limits

❌ Not Covered

  • Damage to your own car
  • Theft of your own vehicle
  • Fire, flood, or natural disaster damage to your car
  • Your own medical expenses (unless add-on purchased)

Choosing Right

Third Party vs Comprehensive — Which Do You Need?

Third Party Only

  • Meets the legal minimum requirement
  • Lower premium
  • Good fit for older cars with low resale value
  • No cover for your own car at all

Comprehensive

  • Includes third party + own-damage cover
  • Protects against theft, fire, accidents, natural disasters
  • Better fit for newer or high-value cars
  • Higher premium, more complete protection
