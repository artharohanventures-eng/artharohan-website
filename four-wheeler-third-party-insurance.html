Choosing Right

Third-Party vs Comprehensive

This is the real choice for car insurance — not a "which lender" question like a loan, but "how much of your own risk do you want covered."

Third-Party Only

  • Legally mandatory — driving without it is a punishable offence
  • Covers damage or injury you cause to others and their property
  • Rate is fixed by IRDAI — same across every insurer
  • Does not cover your own car at all, even if you're not at fault

Comprehensive

  • Includes third-party cover plus own-damage protection
  • Covers theft, fire, accident damage, and natural disasters to your car
  • Premium varies by insurer, IDV, vehicle age, and add-ons chosen
  • Only cover type where No Claim Bonus actually reduces your premium

Personal Accident (owner-driver, ₹15L)✔ Compulsory by law*✔ Compulsory by law*

⚠️ If your car is financed or under 3-4 years old, comprehensive is usually worth the extra cost — the payout on a total loss or theft claim can be many times your annual premium. Tell us your car's age and value and we'll tell you honestly whether comprehensive makes sense for you.

*Personal Accident cover for the owner-driver (₹15 lakh) is compulsory under the Motor Vehicles Act and is bundled into your premium by default — you can only skip it if you already hold an equivalent PA cover elsewhere and formally declare it.

A concrete example: a car with a ₹6,00,000 IDV and 5+ year NCB might cost around ₹3,416/year for third-party only (1000-1500cc slab), versus roughly ₹9,750-15,050/year comprehensive depending on insurer, own-damage discount, and add-ons — try the calculator above with your own numbers. If that car is stolen, third-party pays you nothing toward replacing it; comprehensive could pay close to its ₹6,00,000 IDV. That trade-off is exactly what the recommendation tool above is built to answer for your specific car.

No Claim Bonus

Your NCB Ladder — And Why It's Worth Protecting

No Claim Bonus is a discount on your own-damage premium for every claim-free year — it does not apply to the third-party portion. It resets to zero the moment you make an own-damage claim, no matter how many years you'd built up. This slab structure is set by IRDAI and applies across two-wheelers and cars alike.

NCB belongs to you, not your car or your insurer — if you sell your car or switch insurers, you can carry your NCB forward with a certificate, instead of losing it. Always request this before closing an old policy.

What Actually Moves Your Premium

What Insurers Actually Check

Engine Capacity

Third-party rates step up in fixed IRDAI slabs by CC. Own-damage premium also generally rises with engine size and vehicle value.

Vehicle Age & IDV

IDV (Insured Declared Value) depreciates every year — a 5-year-old car has a lower IDV, and therefore a lower own-damage premium, than a new one.

No Claim Bonus

Every claim-free year raises your NCB discount on own-damage premium, up to 50% at 5+ years — resetting to zero on any own-damage claim.

Insurer & Make

The exact own-damage discount an insurer offers depends on their internal risk model for your car's specific make and model — this is the one factor we genuinely cannot price for you without a real check.
