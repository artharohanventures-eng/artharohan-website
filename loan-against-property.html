Choosing Right

LAP vs Personal Loan vs Home Loan

All three can fund different needs, but the collateral, cost, and risk are very different. Knowing which one actually fits your situation matters more than chasing the lowest headline rate.

Loan Against Property

  • Secured by a property you already own
  • Lower rate than personal loan, larger amounts possible
  • Any end-use — business, medical, education, consolidation
  • Risk: property can be repossessed on serious default

Personal Loan

  • Fully unsecured — no property at risk
  • Higher rate, smaller amounts, faster disbursal
  • Better fit for smaller, shorter-term needs

Home Loan

  • Specifically for buying or constructing a home
  • Lowest rate of the three — end-use restricted
  • Cannot be used for business or personal expenses

If a personal loan or business loan can cover your need at an amount you're comfortable with, that's usually the lower-risk choice. LAP makes sense when you need a larger amount at a lower rate than unsecured options offer — not as a default first choice.

Eligibility

What Lenders Actually Check

For Salaried Applicants

  • Stable employment, usually 2+ years including current job
  • Age at loan maturity typically capped around 60-65
  • CIBIL score 700+ preferred for best rates
  • Salary slips and bank statements as income proof

For Self-Employed Applicants

  • ITR for last 2-3 years typically required
  • Business vintage of 3+ years generally expected
  • Bank statement cash flow and GST returns reviewed
  • Common route for Delhi NCR shop owners and traders needing working capital

What the Property Must Clear

  • Clear, undisputed title in the applicant's name
  • No existing loan against the same property (or it must be closed/transferred first)
  • Lender-approved valuation before sanction
  • Residential properties typically get better LTV than commercial

Documents Needed (Both)

  • PAN Card and Aadhaar Card
  • Income proof (salary slips or ITR)
  • Bank statements — usually last 6 months
  • Property title deed and ownership documents
Honest Before Anything Else

When We'll Tell You "Not Yet"

This is the one loan on our site where the collateral is often someone's home. If any of these apply to you, we'll say so clearly — even though it means no business for us that day.

⚠️ You're Considering This for a Short-Term or Discretionary Need

Putting your home on the line for a wedding, vacation, or short-term expense carries a risk that usually isn't worth it. A personal loan, even at a higher rate, keeps the property out of the equation.

⚠️ It's Your Only Home

If this is your primary residence with no backup housing, we'll ask hard questions before recommending you put it up as collateral — the downside here isn't just financial.

⚠️ EMI Would Cross 40-45% of Income

Same rule as every loan on this site, but the stakes are higher here — a missed payment risks a property, not just your credit score. We'll say so, and the calculator above will flag it.

⚠️ It's for a Business Purpose — Check Prepayment Terms Carefully

RBI's rule against foreclosure charges on floating-rate loans mainly protects individual, non-business borrowing. If you're taking this loan for business use, foreclosure charges may still apply — confirm this explicitly before signing, don't assume the same protection as a home loan.

How LAP Approval Actually Works

Don't assume the same zero-prepayment-charge protection as a home loan applies — confirm this in writing for your specific loan purpose.
