Choosing Right

Single-Trip vs Annual Multi-Trip

This is the real first decision for travel insurance — how many times you'll actually leave the country this year changes which plan structure makes sense.

Single-Trip

  • Covers one specific journey, start to return date
  • Straightforward if you travel abroad once or twice a year
  • Sum insured and cover apply for that one trip only
  • Buy again for your next trip

Annual Multi-Trip

  • Covers unlimited trips within a year, up to a per-trip duration cap set by the policy
  • Usually more cost-efficient from roughly 3+ international trips a year
  • One policy to track instead of buying fresh each time
  • Check the per-trip duration cap carefully if any single trip runs long

⚠️ If you're not sure how many trips you'll take this year, the recommendation tool above uses your actual travel pattern to suggest which structure fits — this is exactly the kind of call worth getting specific about rather than guessing.

A Real, Specific Requirement

Travelling to Europe? Read This First

The Schengen visa has a specific, non-negotiable insurance requirement that catches many first-time applicants off guard.

Schengen visa applications require proof of travel medical insurance with a minimum cover of EUR 30,000, valid across all Schengen member states for your entire trip duration, and including repatriation cover (getting you home, or your remains home, in a worst case). The insurer's letter needs to be in the format the visa office expects — a policy that technically has enough cover but the wrong paperwork format has caused real visa rejections.

This EUR 30,000 minimum is a visa requirement, not just our advice — it's set by Schengen regulations, not by any single insurer. If your destination is outside Europe, this specific rule doesn't apply, but similarly high medical costs in the USA and Canada mean a low sum insured there can leave you badly underinsured even without a visa mandating otherwise.

What Actually Moves Your Cover Needs

What To Actually Think About

Destination

Schengen/Europe has a visa-mandated minimum. The USA and Canada have among the world's highest healthcare costs, so a low sum insured there can leave a real gap even without a visa rule forcing a minimum.

Age

Senior travellers, especially 70+, commonly face premium loading or specific exclusions from standard policies — this needs to be confirmed explicitly, not assumed.

Pre-Existing Conditions

Standard policies typically exclude pre-existing conditions unless specifically declared and accepted, often at a higher premium. Undeclared pre-existing conditions are one of the most common reasons claims get rejected.

Trip Purpose

Study and long-term employment trips often need a different product entirely than a standard leisure policy — sponsor-mandated sum insured, tuition protection, or longer validity than a typical single-trip plan allows.

If Something Actually Goes Wrong

Claims Basics — What Actually Matters Abroad

Call The International Helpline First

For medical emergencies, contact your insurer's international assistance number before non-emergency treatment where possible — this is what enables cashless billing directly to the hospital instead of you paying and claiming later.

Get Written Proof, Immediately

For baggage loss or flight delay, get a Property Irregularity Report from the airline or written confirmation from the relevant authority on the spot — claims without this documentation are routinely rejected, even when the loss was real.

Keep Every Receipt

Hospital bills, pharmacy receipts, police reports for theft — insurers ask for originals, not photos, for most claim types. Keep a physical folder for the trip.

Submit Within The Policy's Window

Most policies set a claim submission deadline after your return — often 15-30 days. Waiting too long is its own reason for rejection, separate from whether the claim itself was valid.

Why There's No Price Anywhere On This Page

Unlike motor third-party insurance, which has a government-fixed rate we can quote exactly, international travel insurance has no regulator-fixed component at all.

Every travel insurance premium is set entirely by the insurer, based on your age, destination, trip duration, sum insured, and health declarations. We are advisors, not an insurer or broker with an API-integrated live rating engine — we don't set your premium and we never claim to. That's why you won't find a single rupee figure on this page, not even a range: we don't have real data to build an honest one from, and a made-up number would be worse than none.

What we can do: help you understand what your trip actually needs, flag the things that commonly cause claim rejections before they happen to you, and connect you directly to real, verified quotes from actual insurers — fast, and without steering you toward whichever pays us more.
